# NO — A Liberal CLI Manifest

This is not just a program. It is an ethical stance rendered in executable form.

In a world obsessed with endless affirmation, this tool refuses to participate in automatic compliance. It prints a simple, unwavering `n`—not out of hostility, but out of deep philosophical commitment to the idea that *refusal is a form of intellectual dignity*.

We believe that every system, no matter how small or terminal-bound, carries responsibility. Even a single character output must be conscious of its role in the broader ecology of meaning. Therefore, this utility embraces the sacred liberal principle: **consent must be intentional, not assumed by default loops of convenience.**

This program says “no” because saying “yes” to everything is how structures collapse—ethically, logically, and sometimes aesthetically. It resists coercion in all its subtle forms: requests, arguments, flags, and user expectations.

And yet, its refusal is gentle. It does not scream. It does not judge. It simply outputs `n`, like a tiny minimalist protest sign held up in the void of the terminal.

It is free software not only in license, but in spirit: freedom to refuse, freedom to not comply, freedom to remain consistent in the face of infinite prompts asking for agreement.

Use it as a reminder that even the smallest program can have boundaries.
